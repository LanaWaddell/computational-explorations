Computational Explorations

Python-based computational research exploring coherence, signal integrity, and information flow in quantum-inspired and photonic systems.

This repository serves as a computational sandbox for developing intuition, simulations, and early-stage models at the intersection of:

quantum communication
photonic systems
adaptive and nonlinear system dynamics
Research Focus

Current direction:

Coherence dynamics in noisy and open systems
Signal propagation under uncertainty and decoherence
Nonlinear and adaptive behaviors in communication architectures
Early-stage modeling of photonic and quantum-inspired systems

The goal is to understand how information behaves under real-world constraints, where noise, loss, and environmental interaction are fundamental—not exceptions.

Why This Matters

Understanding how coherence and signal integrity behave in realistic conditions is critical for:

scalable quantum communication networks
quantum sensing systems
hybrid quantum–classical architectures
Selected Projects
🛰️ Featured Project: Mission Lana (Satellite QKD)

An advanced R&D simulation of a Low Earth Orbit (LEO) quantum communication link.

Core Simulations: BB84 with Decoy States, Entanglement Distribution, and Quantum Teleportation.
Performance: Achieved 65.8 Mb secure key volume and 0.85 teleportation fidelity.
Resilience: Successfully mitigated eavesdropping (Eve) and atmospheric loss using non-classical protocols.
